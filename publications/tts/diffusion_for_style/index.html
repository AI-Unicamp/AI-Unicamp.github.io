Diffusion-based Approach to Style Modelling in Expressive TTS

Paper: Not available yet

Authors: Leonardo B. de M. M. Marques, Paula D. P. Costa, Lucas H. Ueda, Flávio O. Simões, Mário Uliani Neto, Fernando O. Runstein, Edson J. Nagle, Bianca Dal Bó

ABSTRACT

In this article, diffusion models are aggregated to an end-to-end text-to-speech sytem, to learn a distribution of reference speaking styles in an unsupervised manner. Through the use of a noising-denoising process of the reference embedding, and a combined denoising and spectrogram reconstruction loss, style transfer and the conditioning of the acoustic model are achieved. The proposed approach improves several objective metrics. A perceptual MUSHRA evaluation shows a 5 point increment on the mean of naturalness ratings and a paired comparison (ABX test) on style transfer reveals preference of the raters (43%) of our proposed approach over state-of-the-art models (29%).
